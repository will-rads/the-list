# The Secret Society — Public Knowledge Brief

> Sources: app store listing, company website (the-secret-society.com), SEELE Magazine founder interview with Romain Fourel, Lucas Stamm's Dubai influencer-platform analysis, CORQ industry write-up.

A reference doc for the Friday/Saturday founder meeting. Use this to set our floor for product decisions: what TSS does, where it's strong, where it's weak, and what we should and shouldn't copy.

**Why this matters for us:** Radwan and Dima already do the WhatsApp-list version of this — same exact origin as Fourel had. The 300-name Dima list **is the asset** Fourel had to build for years. We're starting where he was in year 3.

## 3. How it actually works

### For influencers (member side)
- Free to join.
- Apply via iOS / Android.
- **Vetting uses something they call "META"** — analyzes Instagram for *engagement levels, follower quality, content appeal.* Not just a raw follower count.
- Once in, you browse barter "experiences" by category: dining, nightlife (VIP tables), beach/pool clubs, gyms, wellness, beauty, clinics, retail, attractions.
- Book it like a reservation.
- **Post requirement:** the recurring complaint from app reviews is the **4 mandatory Stories** rule. That's the consideration the venue extracts.
- *"Choose only the experiences you want, on your terms. No long contracts, no pressure."* — they pitch flexibility, but the 4-Story rule is the friction point.
- A nice retention hook: **you can bring guests** (per Lucas Stamm).

### For brands / venues
- Pay TSS — *"a fraction of traditional PR costs."* Exact pricing hidden behind a contact form.
- TSS pitch to brands:
  - *"Tap into a global network at a fraction of traditional PR costs"*
  - **Smart filters** to *"define who you want and how you want to be promoted"* — venues can target by audience type, location, follower band.
  - *"Complimentary collaboration — creators showcase your venue through curated content"*
  - *"Dozens of voices amplifying your brand at once"*
  - **"Save time — We manage all pre and post creator communication."** ← this is the actual product. TSS isn't a marketplace, it's a **concierge with a marketplace UI**.

### Verticals (multi-category from day 1)
F&B (casual → fine dining), nightlife (VIP tables), beach clubs, pool clubs, gyms, wellness lounges, clinics, beauty salons, retail stores, attractions.

### Growth model
- *"Our growth has been entirely through word-of-mouth… members become our best advocates."*
- They don't run ads. They rely on members posting Stories (free distribution) and other members recruiting.

## 4. The market context — Dubai's regulatory wedge

This is the single most important reason TSS works in Dubai that **doesn't apply in Lebanon**:

- Dubai requires a **social media influencer license** — AED 11,900 (~£2,596 / ~$3,240) **per year** to legally do paid promotions.
- Micro-influencers can't afford it → they get pushed to **barter** as a workaround.
- TSS is essentially the legal escape valve for the entire micro tier in the UAE.

**For Lebanon:** no equivalent license requirement. That's a double-edged sword:
- ✅ More influencers can participate freely — bigger potential pool.
- ❌ **Lower switching cost / lower defensibility.** A second app can copy us easily.

→ Our moat **must** come from **venue exclusivity contracts**, not regulatory capture.

## 6. Public criticism / weaknesses (what we should exploit)

Most cited complaints in app reviews and on Reddit / TikTok:

1. **4-Story rule clutters feeds.** Influencers feel like walking ads — every post is obviously a collab. Devalues the influencer's account *and* makes the brand placement feel cheap.
2. **Briefings can be generic.** Same brief across many creators → same-looking content → diminishing returns for venues.
3. **No transparency on pricing.** Venues have to call a salesperson to even know what it costs.
4. **No real proof-of-quality scoring on the post itself** — once you've completed your 4 Stories, you've fulfilled the deal whether they're beautiful or trash.
5. **Hard to cancel / commit-and-disappear.** Mentioned in some negative reviews.

These are real gaps. Our v1 product positioning can hit several of them directly.

## 7. What we should copy verbatim

- ✅ **Barter model** — free for influencer, brand pays. Don't fight this; it's industry standard.
- ✅ **Quality-based vetting** (engagement, content quality, not just follower count). Build our own simple META-equivalent: scrape public Instagram metrics + manual override by the three of us.
- ✅ **Multi-vertical from day 1** — clubs, restaurants, beach clubs (Lebanon has them), gyms, beauty salons. Don't be just-nightlife.
- ✅ **Concierge layer** — TSS's real product is *"we manage all pre and post creator communication."* Dima literally does this already.
- ✅ **App-first** (iOS + Android). Web-only would fail.
- ✅ **Smart filters for brands** — let the venue specify *what kind* of crowd they want.
- ✅ **Word-of-mouth growth via members.** Build in a referral mechanic.

## 8. What we should improve / differentiate (our wedges)

### Wedge 1 — Limited-spots drops (Dima's idea)
TSS lets brands run an "experience" with no public spot limit. Ours runs as **drops**: *"20 spots tonight at Kee Beirut, $50 bundle, claim before midnight."*
- Creates scarcity → FOMO → claim rate.
- Better content output (20 well-dressed people at once = a vibe, not 200 randos drifting through).
- Easier for the venue to forecast cost.

### Wedge 2 — Story-quality over story-quantity
Drop the "4 mandatory Stories" rule. Replace with **1-2 high-quality required posts + bonus credits for aesthetic quality** (we rate it; better ratings = better future drops). This solves the #1 complaint.

### Wedge 3 — Transparent pricing for venues
A self-serve "create your bundle" dashboard with our cut visible up front. Like Shopify did to retail. Faster sales cycle than TSS's contact-form approach.

### Wedge 4 — Local payment rails
Whish, OMT, USD cash. Don't try to be Stripe-first in Lebanon. This is a non-trivial advantage over a Dubai-based competitor parachuting in.

### Wedge 5 — Bilingual brand voice
Arabic + English copy. TSS is English-only. Lebanese brand voice can be Arabizi-flavored.

### Wedge 6 — DJ / events layer (Radwan's later phase)
TSS doesn't do DJ bookings or event-tier promotion. This is genuine net-new product surface for phase 2, defended by Radwan's network.

## 10. The one-paragraph competitive summary

The Secret Society works because Fourel spent **3 years manually doing what Dima already does**, then automated it into an app that matches barter offers across 10 verticals to a quality-vetted member base, while quietly being a concierge service that handles all the creator communication for the brand. They are **800 venues, 100k members, 8 cities, 60 staff, shipping a new city every 2 months.** Their weaknesses are obvious (4-Story spam, no public pricing, generic briefs, no quality bar on output) and exploitable. Their threat to us is real (Beirut is plausibly on their 2026 roadmap). **The play is: ship in 60-90 days, lock anchor-venue exclusivity, differentiate on limited-drops + post-quality + bilingual + local payments, and use Dima's list as the cold-start that took Fourel three unpaid years to build.**
